Investigative Summary
---------------------
At 2025-08-02 07:07:41UTC user "tabadmin" logged onto host 	SL-DC-01 from an internal IP address of 10.100.0[.]6 which is a Huntress unmanaged host.

At 2025-08-02 07:28 UTC, Huntress detected an adversary using "WinRAR.exe" to archive and password-protect numerous sensitive document types. This was part of a broader series of interactive commands, including reconnaissance with "whoami.exe" and an attempt to uninstall the Huntress agent. The presence of "rdpclip.exe" indicates the attacker was connected via a Remote Desktop session.

These actions are consistent with pre-ransomware activity. The threat actor accessed backup software, likely to disable or delete recovery points, and have download data exfiltration tool. The attempt to remove the Huntress agent, is a clear defence evasion tactic designed to prolong unauthorized access and evade detection.

The attacker has active control of this system, posing an immediate risk of widespread data theft and business disruption. If successful, the attacker could encrypt critical files, leak stolen data publicly, and demand a ransom, leading to significant operational and financial damage.

Huntress has mass isolated the organisation to prevent further compromise. Please carry out assisted remediation, rotate all user credentials and activate your incident response plan. If the mass isolation is lifted without determining the root cause of this intrusion, the threat actor access will maintain control over this system.
