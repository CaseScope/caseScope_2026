Investigative Summary
---------------------
At 2025-09-30 20:03:17 UTC, Microsoft Defender Antivirus identified the following suspicious behavior "Trojan:JS/Trickbot.S!MSR" in the following location on the endpoint: Dannis ~ C:\ProgramData\controlScr.hta.

Huntress detected remnants of malicious software on the endpoint. In this case, artifacts were observed indicating suspicious files (e.g., script or executable references in temporary directories, startup items, or scheduled tasks) that are no longer present on the system. From our investigation, we noted the malicious .hta file was successfully blocked and quarantined by Defender. Secondarily, during the investigation, a script designed to disable Windows Defender was detected. These Malware artifacts are indicators of compromise prior to the Huntress agent being installed, hindering further investigation.

These remnants typically occur when:
- Malware cleans itself up after execution to evade detection
- Antivirus or other tools partially remove a threat but leave behind persistence entries
- Installers or droppers create scheduled tasks, registry keys, or startup items that remain even after the payload is deleted

Although no active payload was detected at this time, these artifacts suggest that malware execution likely occurred on the endpoint. Remnants can still represent risk if additional persistence or secondary payloads were deployed during the initial compromise. As such, Huntress SOC has identified these items needing removal.
