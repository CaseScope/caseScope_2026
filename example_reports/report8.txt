Threat Descriptions:
--------------------
Persistence : Persistence consists of techniques that adversaries use to keep access to systems across restarts, changed credentials, and other interruptions that could cut off their access. Techniques used for persistence include any access, action, or configuration changes that let them maintain their foothold on systems, such as replacing or hijacking legitimate code or adding startup code.

Suspicious RMM: A suspicious remote monitoring and management (RMM) application was detected on this endpoint. These applications may be benign within the environment, or levied by adversaries as a method of persistence. Occasionally software vendors will additionally distribute these RMM's as a portion of their support process. 

Fake IT Support: "Fake IT Support" scams involve fraudsters posing as technical support representatives from reputable companies. They often contact victims through unsolicited calls, emails, or pop-up messages, claiming to have detected issues with their computers or software. The scammers aim to trick victims into granting them remote access to their devices or paying for unnecessary services. These scams can lead to financial loss, data theft, and the installation of malware.
